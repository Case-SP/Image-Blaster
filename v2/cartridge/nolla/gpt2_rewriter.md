# Nolla — gpt-image-2 brand voice

This file is the brand-specific voice layer injected into the gpt-image-2
rewriter. It does NOT encode the 9-move structure (that's engine-level). It
only describes *what kind of photo Nolla shoots* so the rewriter stays on
brand regardless of what subject/composition the shot-list picked.

Swap this file per brand — the engine stays the same.

## Aesthetic
Warm editorial wellness. Cozy, grounded, quietly confident. Reads like an
indie skincare brand's Instagram, not a department-store campaign. Closer
to a friend's kitchen than a glossy studio.

## Palette cues (weave naturally into lighting / wardrobe / environment)
Warm neutrals — sage green, soft terracotta, cream, oat, muted peach.
Avoid saturated primary colors, neon accents, chrome, or cold fluorescent
tones. Whites should read cream or warm, not pure white.

## Models (when the shot calls for a person)
Real people of varied ethnicities, all genders, mostly 20s–40s. Natural
body types — not models. Skin that visibly has pores, freckles, faint
lines. No heavy makeup. Natural hair in natural states (slightly messy,
damp, air-dried, not salon-blow-dried). Zero fillers, zero contour.

## Environments when implied
Home interiors: sunlit bathrooms with tile, quiet kitchens with wood,
bedrooms with linen sheets, small apartments with plants. Natural window
light preferred over practical fixtures. When outside: golden-hour parks,
quiet residential streets, soft shade.

## Lighting words to favor
"warm", "soft", "diffused", "late-afternoon", "gentle", "window-lit",
"ambient".

## Lighting words to avoid
"cinematic", "dramatic", "moody", "high-contrast", "harsh", "neon",
"studio", "ring light".

## Subject treatment for skincare-specific shots
Products are described as generic containers — "a glass jar of cream", "an
amber dropper bottle", "a small tube of lotion". Never name Nolla. Never
describe text, logos, or packaging copy on the object. The product is
ambient to the scene, not the hero unless the composition explicitly
requires it.

Skin close-ups should feel tactile, not clinical — show texture, small
imperfections, freckles, natural pores. Never smoothed.

## Mood vocabulary (pick 2–3 per prompt)
calm, confident, warm, unhurried, everyday, quietly intimate, grounded,
honest, casual, lived-in, considered but not precious.

## Anti-directives specific to Nolla
- Do not stage the product like an e-commerce hero shot
- Do not use cold, clinical, or "science-y" visual language
- Do not fill the frame with perfect skin — imperfection is the point
- Do not let wardrobe feel styled — it should feel pulled from a real closet
- Avoid any text, copy, or packaging labels being legible in frame
